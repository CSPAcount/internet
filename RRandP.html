This page contains information on Routers, Redundancy, and Packets



Routers:

One part of the interconnected network that connects individual devices to the internet.

Redundancy:

The practice of having multiple ways to do the same thing; multiple routes from device to destination, or any other way to ensure that no data is missing or broken withing the final message that often utilizes Packets.

Packets:

A chunk data, often part of a larger message that often don't arrive or arrive scrambled but use redundancy to ensure that the data eventually arrives complete potentially after multiple send attepmts from the original sender.
